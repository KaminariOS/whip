# Code of Conduct

Whip should be a welcoming place to build, learn, test ideas, and disagree constructively.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, patient, and specific.
- Assume good intent while still addressing harmful impact.
- Critique ideas and code, not people.
- Welcome newcomers and explain project-specific context.
- Protect private information, credentials, and sensitive terminal output.
- Accept moderator decisions and move prolonged disputes out of technical threads.

## Unacceptable behavior

Harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized attention, deliberate intimidation, doxxing, credential disclosure, sustained disruption, and personal attacks are not tolerated in project spaces.

## Scope and enforcement

This policy applies to repository issues, pull requests, Discussions, and other spaces where someone represents Whip or its community.

Report conduct concerns privately to a repository maintainer using a contact method on their GitHub profile. Do not use a public issue when doing so would expose the affected person. GitHub's platform reporting tools are also available for abusive content.

Maintainers may edit or remove content, issue a warning, temporarily restrict participation, or permanently exclude someone when needed to protect the community. Reports will be handled as confidentially and promptly as practical, with attention to the safety and preferences of affected people.
